Security

An honest look at how we protect your data.

Raceplace is a small, focused service built on trusted cloud infrastructure. We take the security of your data seriously and aim to be transparent about the measures we have in place. Below is an overview of our approach.

Encryption in Transit

All data transmitted between your device, Garmin Connect, and our servers is protected using HTTPS/TLS. This means your activity data and personal information are encrypted while they travel over the internet.

Encryption at Rest

Data stored on our servers is encrypted using the built-in encryption capabilities of Microsoft Azure. This protects your data even when it is not actively being accessed.

Secure Cloud Infrastructure

Raceplace runs on Microsoft Azure, a globally recognised cloud platform that maintains a wide range of industry security certifications and compliance standards.

Access Controls

Access to production systems and personal data is restricted to authorised personnel only, following the principle of least privilege. We don’t give anyone more access than they need to do their job.

Dependency Management

We regularly review and update our software dependencies to address known security vulnerabilities and keep the service running on supported, patched versions.

Monitoring & Logging

We use logging and monitoring tools to detect unusual activity. This helps us identify and respond to potential issues quickly.

Reporting a Security Concern

If you believe you have found a security vulnerability in Raceplace, or if you suspect your account or data may have been compromised, please let us know immediately.

Contact us at [SUPPORT_EMAIL] with the subject line “Security concern”. Please include as much detail as you can so we can investigate promptly.

We take all reports seriously and will acknowledge your message within 2 working days. We appreciate responsible disclosure and will work with you to address any confirmed issues.

A Note on Honesty

Raceplace is a small service, and we won’t pretend otherwise. We don’t claim to have the security resources of a large enterprise. What we do commit to is:

  • Using reputable, well-secured cloud infrastructure.
  • Following established security best practices appropriate to the size and nature of our service.
  • Being transparent when things go wrong and communicating promptly with affected users.
  • Continuously improving our security posture as the service grows.

If you have questions about our security practices, we’re happy to discuss them. Reach out at [SUPPORT_EMAIL].
