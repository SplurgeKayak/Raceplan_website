Privacy Policy

How Raceplace collects, uses, and protects your data.

Last updated: [DATE]

1. Introduction

Raceplace (“the App”) is operated by [COMPANY_NAME] (“we”, “us”, “our”). Raceplace is part of the Boathouse digital canoe & kayak racing platform.

The App allows paddlers to compete in virtual races, track training progress, and appear on leaderboards by connecting their Garmin Connect account so we can read their paddle activity data.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and the choices you have. It applies to all users of the App and this website.

If you have any questions, please contact us at [DATA_PROTECTION_EMAIL].

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Account & Contact Data

When you create a Raceplace account or contact us, we may collect:

  • Your name or display name
  • Email address
  • Any information you provide when you contact support

Source: provided directly by you.

2.2 Garmin Activity Data

When you connect your Garmin Connect account, we access the following categories of data through the Garmin Connect API:

  • Activity metadata: activity type, date and time, duration, distance, pace, and speed.
  • Performance metrics (where available): heart rate, power, cadence, and stroke rate.
  • Location data (if enabled by you on your device): GPS route and track used to calculate race positions and course data.
  • Basic account identifiers: Garmin user ID or similar technical identifier, and optional profile information such as display name or profile image that Garmin makes available.

Source: Garmin Connect, with your authorisation.

2.3 Device & Usage Data

We may automatically collect limited technical information to keep the App running smoothly, including:

  • Device type and operating system version
  • App version
  • Crash reports and error logs
  • Aggregated, anonymised usage statistics (e.g., feature usage counts)

Source: your device and our app infrastructure.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your data for the following purposes:

  • Core features: to calculate and display race results, rankings, leaderboards, and training insights.
  • Operating the service: to run, maintain, and improve the App, including fixing bugs and optimising performance.
  • Fair play: to detect cheating or anomalous activity at a high level in order to maintain fair competition.
  • Support & communication: to respond to your enquiries and send you important service-related messages (e.g., changes to terms or outage notices).
  • Safety & security: to protect the security and integrity of the App and its users.
  • Legal obligations: to comply with applicable laws, regulations, or valid legal processes.

We do not sell your personal data to anyone.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Where UK or EU data-protection law applies, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract: processing necessary to provide the Raceplace service to you (e.g., calculating race results from your activity data).
  • Legitimate interests: processing necessary for our legitimate interests, provided these are not overridden by your rights. This includes improving the App, maintaining fair play, and ensuring security.
  • Consent: where you actively choose to connect your Garmin account or enable optional features. You can withdraw consent at any time by disconnecting your account or contacting us.
  • Legal obligation: where processing is required to comply with a legal requirement.

5. Data Retention

We keep your data for as long as reasonably necessary to provide the service and fulfil the purposes described in this policy, or as required by law.

  • Active accounts: we retain your data while your account is active and you remain connected to Garmin.
  • After disconnection or deletion request: if you disconnect your Garmin account and request deletion of your data, we will remove or anonymise your identifiable activity data within [X days/months], unless we are required to retain certain information for legal, security, or fraud-prevention reasons.
  • Anonymised data: we may retain fully anonymised, aggregated data (which cannot identify you) indefinitely for analytics and service improvement.

6. Data Sharing & Transfers

We share personal data only to the extent necessary to operate and improve the service, or where required by law. Recipients may include:

  • Cloud hosting providers: Microsoft Azure, which hosts our infrastructure and stores your data.
  • Analytics providers: services that help us understand usage patterns, using anonymised or aggregated data where possible.
  • Email & communication providers: to send you service-related messages.
  • Payment providers: if applicable, to process payments securely.
  • Legal or regulatory authorities: where required by law or valid legal process.

Some of these providers may process data outside the United Kingdom or the European Economic Area. Where this happens, we take reasonable steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place (for example, standard contractual clauses approved by relevant authorities) to protect your data.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

7. Your Rights

Depending on where you are located, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: ask us to correct any inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Deletion: ask us to delete your personal data, subject to any legal obligations we have to retain it.
  • Restriction: ask us to restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Objection: object to processing based on our legitimate interests.
  • Portability: receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format where technically feasible.

To exercise any of these rights, please email us at [DATA_PROTECTION_EMAIL]. We will respond within a reasonable period, and in any case within the timeframes required by applicable law.

If you are in the UK or EU and are not satisfied with how we handle your request, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority (for example, the Information Commissioner’s Office in the UK).

8. Garmin Connection, Disconnect & Deletion

8.1 Connecting Garmin

When you choose to connect your Garmin account, you are redirected to Garmin’s authorisation flow. You grant Raceplace permission to read the activity data categories described in section 2.2. We do not receive or store your Garmin password.

8.2 Disconnecting Garmin

You can disconnect your Garmin account at any time using any of these methods:

  1. In the App: go to your account or settings area and select the option to disconnect Garmin.
  2. Via Garmin: manage your connected apps through your Garmin Connect account settings.
  3. Contact us: email [SUPPORT_EMAIL] and we will disconnect your account on your behalf.

Once disconnected, we will no longer import new activity data from Garmin. Activity data already imported may remain in our systems until you request its deletion or it is removed in accordance with our retention practices (see section 5).

8.3 Requesting Data Deletion

To request deletion of your personal data:

  1. Email [SUPPORT_EMAIL] with the subject line “Delete my data”.
  2. Include enough information for us to identify your account (e.g., the email address you used to sign up).
  3. We will confirm receipt and process your request within [X working days].

Upon deletion, we will remove or anonymise your identifiable data. We may retain limited information where we have a legal obligation or legitimate need (for example, records of transactions, fraud-prevention data, or aggregated anonymised statistics).

9. Security Measures

We take reasonable and appropriate measures to protect your data, consistent with the size and nature of our service. These include:

  • Encryption in transit: all data transmitted between your device, Garmin, and our servers is protected using HTTPS/TLS.
  • Encryption at rest: data stored on our servers is encrypted using the built-in encryption features of our cloud platform (Microsoft Azure).
  • Access controls: we restrict access to personal data to authorised personnel on a need-to-know basis, following the principle of least privilege.
  • Monitoring: we use logging and monitoring to detect and respond to potential security incidents.
  • Dependency management: we regularly update and patch software dependencies to address known vulnerabilities.

No system can guarantee absolute security. If you believe your data or account may have been compromised, please contact us immediately at [SUPPORT_EMAIL].
